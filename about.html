About The Pulse

Economics journalism from the ground level

Our Mission

The Pulse UBC was founded on a simple belief: the best economic journalism happens when you talk to real people, not just experts and policymakers. While traditional economics coverage focuses on policy papers and market data, we believe the most important stories are found in conversations with the people experiencing economic change firsthand.

Every week, we publish in-depth reporting that combines rigorous economic analysis with ground-level journalism. We interview workers, business owners, students, and community members to understand how policies, market forces, and economic trends shape daily life in Vancouver and beyond.

From wealth inequality to monetary policy, from housing affordability to labor markets, we explore the economics that matters, told through the voices of those who live it.

Our Writers

Sarah Chen

Sarah is a third-year Economics student at UBC originally from San Francisco. Her reporting focuses on wealth inequality, housing policy, and the intersection of economics and social justice. Before co-founding The Pulse, she covered student housing issues for The Ubyssey and interned at the San Francisco Federal Reserve.

Michael Rivera

Michael is a fourth-year student studying Economics and Political Science at UBC. His work explores labor markets, monetary policy, and the gig economy. He's particularly interested in how macroeconomic policy affects workers and small businesses. When he's not reporting, he's analyzing central bank communications and market trends.

Contributing Writers

We welcome collaborations with students, economists, and journalists who share our commitment to rigorous, accessible economic reporting. If you're interested in contributing to The Pulse, reach out to us.
